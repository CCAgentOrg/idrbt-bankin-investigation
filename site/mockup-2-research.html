00. Executive Summary

RBI created .bank.in as a trust anchor — a domain suffix that tells citizens "this website is genuinely your bank." But the portal that issues these banking domains was built without a public tender, without a published security baseline, and without any vulnerability disclosure program. For over a year, it leaked every credential it held.

Key finding: The IDRBT Domain Registration Portal exposed 33+ unauthenticated API endpoints, leaking bcrypt password hashes, PII, and device fingerprints of 5,576 bank employees. The system meant to secure banking was itself the weakest link.

The portal was built by IKCON Technologies in a single-source award — no tender, no RFP, no competitive process — in direct violation of IDRBT's own procurement handbook. IKCON held 22 employee accounts including 3 with global Super Admin access.

I. Security Claims vs. Reality

IDRBT's published Privacy & Security Policy makes specific claims about security auditing, authentication, and data protection. Our investigation found every claim to be false.

❌ Claim #1 — Audited for vulnerabilities
The policy claims the site "was audited for known application-level vulnerabilities before the launch." Our investigation found 33+ unauthenticated API endpoints exposing user database, billing records, system config, and DSC proxy — a comprehensive failure no competent audit could have missed.
Source: registrar.idrbt.ac.in Privacy & Security Policy
❌ Claim #2 — Content is authenticated
The policy states "Content is authenticated." Accessing every discovered endpoint required zero authentication — no login, no API key, no session cookie, no token.
❌ Claim #3 — Procedural safeguards
The policy pledges compliance with applicable laws. Exposing bcrypt password hashes and PII of 5,576 bank employees violates the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, and RBI's own cybersecurity framework (RBI/2023-24/90).
Conclusion: IDRBT's published security policy is a work of fiction. Three specific claims made to the public were verifiably false at the time of investigation.
